Rules

How Chess Works

Chess is a game for two players on an 8x8 board. Each player starts with 16 pieces. The goal is to trap the other player's King so it can't escape. That's called checkmate, and you win!

The Pieces

PieceHow It Moves
KingOne square in any direction. You must keep your King safe!
QueenAny number of squares in any direction (up, down, left, right, or diagonal). The most powerful piece.
RookAny number of squares, but only straight (up, down, left, or right).
BishopAny number of squares, but only diagonally.
KnightMoves in an "L" shape: two squares one way, then one square to the side. The only piece that can jump over others.
PawnMoves forward one square (or two on its first move). Captures diagonally. If it reaches the other end, it evolves into a Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight (you choose)!

Important Terms

  • Check means a King is being attacked. You MUST get out of check on your next move.
  • Checkmate means a King is in check and can't escape. Game over!
  • Stalemate means it's your turn but you have no legal moves (and you're NOT in check). It's a draw.

Pokémon Piece Rules

In Pokémon Chess, each chess piece has rules about which Pokémon can fill that role:

PieceWhich Pokémon Can Be This Piece?
KingAny Pokémon! Pick your favorite.
QueenMust be a Legendary, Mythical, Ultra Beast, or Paradox Pokémon.
RookMust weigh over 300 lbs (and not be Legendary or Ultra Beast).
BishopMust have higher Special Attack than physical Attack.
KnightMust have higher Speed than HP.
PawnAny regular Pokémon (not Legendary, Mythical, Ultra Beast, pseudo-legendary, or Paradox).

Team Colors

Both sides use the same Pokémon team. White's pieces use normal sprites, and Black's pieces use shiny sprites. Look for the colored bar under each Pokémon to tell the teams apart!
